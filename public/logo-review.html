FLICKPICK Logo Concepts

Exploring logo directions for the movie aggregator + social + AI recommendations platform. Tone: cinematic, editorial, premium but approachable. Think A24 meets Letterboxd.

Fonts Instrument Serif + Sora Brand Amber #E05A22 Options 18 concepts (10 icon + 8 wordmark) Date March 18, 2026

Icon + Wordmark Logos

Each concept pairs a custom inline SVG icon with the FLICKPICK wordmark. Icons use the brand amber (#E05A22) and are designed to scale cleanly from 16px to 48px. Shown on dark (header) and light (cream) backgrounds at two sizes.

Wordmark-Only Logos

Pure typographic treatments exploring different font pairings, weights, and visual devices. No icon, just the name.

Option A Classic Wordmark
Dark Background Header / 20px FLICKPICK. Hero / 48px FLICKPICK.
Light Background Header / 20px FLICKPICK. Hero / 48px FLICKPICK.
Rationale: The most direct evolution of the current logo. Clean, confident uppercase tracking with Sora's geometric precision. The gold period acts as a subtle punctuation mark that feels intentional and distinctive without overdesigning. This is the safest option and would be the easiest to recognize at small sizes. Works well for favicons (just the dot) and social avatars.
Option B Split Treatment
Dark Background Header / 20px FlickPICK Hero / 48px FlickPICK
Light Background Header / 20px FlickPICK Hero / 48px FlickPICK
Rationale: Separates the two words semantically by pairing Instrument Serif ("Flick" = the movie) with Sora bold ("PICK" = the action/choice). The serif-to-sans contrast creates visual tension and narrative: you watch a flick, then you pick. Gold on the action word reinforces the platform's core value proposition. The mixed case adds editorial sophistication.
Option C Ligature / Connected
Dark Background Header / 20px FLICKPICK Hero / 48px FLICKPICK
Light Background Header / 20px FLICKPICK Hero / 48px FLICKPICK
Rationale: Uses Instrument Serif for an elevated feel. The gold dots replacing the tittles on both "I" letters create a signature detail that becomes instantly recognizable. The tighter kerning where K meets P creates a visual ligature that ties the two words together. At large sizes, the gold dots become a defining brand element, almost like projected light.
Option D Monogram + Wordmark
Dark Background Header / 20px fp FLICKPICK Hero / 48px fp FLICKPICK
Light Background Header / 20px fp FLICKPICK Hero / 48px fp FLICKPICK
Rationale: A two-part system: the "fp" monogram in Instrument Serif sits inside a bordered square, giving it a stamp/seal quality. This mark works standalone for app icons, favicons, and watermarks. The full wordmark accompanies it for header and marketing use. The gold border on the monogram adds just enough brand color without overwhelming. Versatile across all contexts.
Option E Cinematic
Dark Background Header / 20px FLICKPICK Hero / 48px FLICKPICK
Light Background Header / 20px FLICKPICK Hero / 48px FLICKPICK
Rationale: The gold dashed lines above and below the wordmark evoke film strip perforations, creating an instant cinema association without being literal or gimmicky. The pattern is subtle at header size but becomes a distinctive framing device at hero scale. Sora's geometric weight gives it authority while the film strip motif keeps it grounded in the movie space. Strong widescreen/letterbox energy.
Option F Editorial
Dark Background Header / 24px Flick/Pick Hero / 56px Flick/Pick
Light Background Header / 24px Flick/Pick Hero / 56px Flick/Pick
Rationale: Full Instrument Serif italic creates a publication/magazine feel, like a film journal masthead. The gold forward slash separates the two words while adding a modern editorial touch, reminiscent of masthead dividers in print design. Mixed case italic feels sophisticated and literary without being pretentious. This direction leans hardest into the A24 aesthetic and would pair beautifully with long-form reviews and criticism content.
Option G Minimal Modern
Dark Background Header / 22px flickpick Hero / 52px flickpick
Light Background Header / 22px flickpick Hero / 52px flickpick
Rationale: Stripped to essentials. All lowercase Sora in light weight with the "pick" portion in bold gold creates a clean, app-first aesthetic. This direction feels most contemporary and tech-forward, similar to how streaming platforms present themselves. The weight contrast between "flick" (light) and "pick" (bold/gold) maintains readability while the lowercase treatment keeps it casual and approachable. Best option if the brand skews younger.
Option H Gold Accent Slash
Dark Background Header / 20px FLICKPICK Hero / 48px FLICKPICK
Light Background Header / 20px FLICKPICK Hero / 48px FLICKPICK
Rationale: Gold is used in two precise ways: the opening "F" is gold (anchoring the brand color at the start), and a skewed gold slash divides FLICK from PICK. The slash creates a visual cut, like a film splice or editing mark. This balances restraint with distinctiveness. The gold never overwhelms but always registers. The skewed slash also introduces a sense of motion and dynamism to an otherwise rigid uppercase treatment.
